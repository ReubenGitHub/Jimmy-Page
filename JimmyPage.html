Jimmy Page
Musician   -   Songwriter   -   Producer
Biography

Born on the 9th of January 1944, James Patrick Page rose to be one of the most successful musicians of all time as the guitarist and founder of English rock band Led Zeppelin.

Jimmy was born and raised around west London, and first started to teach himself to play guitar when he was 12, after finding a disused Spanish guitar in his home in Epsom. After becoming ill during tours in his early career, Jimmy pursued art and painting, but eventually returned to musical work and joined The Yardbirds in 1966.
After, Jimmy went on to found Led Zeppelin, and later also participated in several other groups, such as The Firm, Coverdale-Page, and The Honeydrippers.

Jimmy Page is particularly well-known for creating iconic guitar riffs and his extraordinary playing styles, which has included using a cello bow on his guitar strings to create droning sounds. Read more about the life and accomplishments of Jimmy Page on Wikipedia and in his autobiography.

Live Performances

Jimmy Page has performed to live audiences on thousands of occasions. Here is a small selection of some particularly notable performances.

Led Zeppelin - Pontiac Silverdome, 1977
Jimmy Page's performance with Led Zeppelin at the Pontiac Silverdome in 1977 had a massive turnout of approximately 77,000 people, which was the highest attendance recorded for a single act show at the time.

Led Zeppelin Celebration Day - O2 Arena, 2007
Nearly 3 decades after Led Zeppelin's drummer, John Bonham, died in 1980, the remaining band members got together with John Bonham's son, Jason Bonham, to perform a Led Zeppelin reunion act at the O2 Arena in 2007.

The Firm - Hammersmith Odeon, 1984
After the end of the Led Zeppelin era, Jimmy Page formed a rock supergroup with Bad Company vocalist Paul Rodgers, Manfred Mann's Earth Band drummer Chris Slade, and Tony Franklin on a fretless bass.
